How Air Purifiers Help with Allergies

Indoor air typically contains 2 to 5 times more pollutants than outdoor air, according to EPA data. Common indoor allergens include pollen tracked in from outdoors, dust mite fecal particles (the primary allergen from dust mites, measuring 10 to 40 microns), pet dander (microscopic skin flakes from cats, dogs, and other animals, measuring 2.5 to 10 microns), and mold spores (3 to 100 microns depending on species).

A True HEPA filter captures 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger. Since all major indoor allergens are significantly larger than 0.3 microns, HEPA filtration is extremely effective at removing them. The key is running the purifier continuously in the rooms where you spend the most time, particularly the bedroom (where you spend 7 to 9 hours per night breathing filtered air).

True HEPA vs. HEPA-Type

Only "True HEPA" or "H13 HEPA" filters meet the 99.97% at 0.3 micron standard. Filters marketed as "HEPA-type," "HEPA-like," or "HEPA-style" do not meet this standard and may capture significantly fewer allergens. Always verify True HEPA certification before purchasing.

Air Purifier Buying Guide for Allergy Sufferers

Prioritize Your Bedroom

If you can only afford one air purifier, put it in your bedroom. You spend a third of your life sleeping, and breathing filtered air for 7 to 9 hours nightly provides the most significant reduction in allergy symptoms. A $99 Levoit Core 300 in the bedroom is more impactful than a $300 purifier in a living room you use for 4 hours a day.

Size Correctly

An undersized purifier will not clean the air fast enough. Use the CADR as two-thirds of room square footage as your minimum. Oversizing is always better because it allows the purifier to run on lower, quieter speeds while still maintaining clean air.

Calculate Total Cost of Ownership

The purchase price is just the beginning. Filter replacement costs add $30 to $100 per year depending on the model. Electricity costs add $2 to $5 per month. A cheap purifier with expensive filters can cost more over 3 years than a premium purifier with affordable filters.

Look for AHAM Verifide Certification

The Association of Home Appliance Manufacturers (AHAM) independently tests and verifies CADR claims. Purifiers with the AHAM Verifide seal have verified performance numbers. Purifiers without it may have inflated or untested claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do air purifiers actually help with allergies?

Yes. Clinical studies show that True HEPA air purifiers reduce airborne allergens by 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger, including pollen, dust mite debris, pet dander, and mold spores. Allergy sufferers consistently report reduced symptoms when using a properly sized HEPA air purifier in their bedroom and main living areas. The EPA, ACAAI, and multiple allergist organizations recommend HEPA air purifiers as part of an allergy management plan.

What size air purifier do I need?

Match the purifier's CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) to your room size. As a rule, the CADR should be at least two-thirds of the room's square footage. For a 300 sq ft bedroom, you need a CADR of at least 200. For a 500 sq ft living room, CADR of 333 or higher. Oversizing is always better than undersizing.

How often should I replace air purifier filters?

Most HEPA filters need replacement every 6 to 12 months depending on usage and air quality. Pre-filters should be cleaned monthly and replaced every 3 months. Carbon/activated charcoal filters for odors typically last 3 to 6 months. Running the purifier 24/7 in a dusty or pet-heavy environment shortens filter life. Always use genuine manufacturer replacement filters.

Are expensive air purifiers better than cheap ones?

Not necessarily. The filtration technology (True HEPA) is the same across price points. Expensive models typically offer larger coverage areas, quieter operation, smart features, better build quality, and lower long-term filter costs. A $100 HEPA purifier filters air just as effectively as a $500 one for its rated room size.

Should I run my air purifier 24/7?

Yes, for best results. Allergens are continuously released into the air from surfaces, fabrics, pets, and outdoor air infiltration. Running the purifier 24/7 on a low or auto setting maintains consistently clean air. The energy cost is minimal, typically $2 to $5 per month on low speed.
